# Page Capabilities

## Public Experience

The public-facing experience allows visitors to understand the product, review its positioning, and begin the onboarding process.

Current public capabilities include:

- product discovery
- plan visibility
- authentication entry points
- legal and trust-related information
- public verification of issued document data

## Authenticated Experience

Authenticated users can access business and account-related workflows through private areas of the platform.

Current private capabilities include:

- account management
- billing visibility
- subscription-related actions
- support workflows
- support ticket lifecycle actions
- invitation-based collaboration
- role-based team collaboration
- light mode interface support
- persistent navigation state in compact sidebar flows

## Platform Experience

Platform administrators can operate cross-company management workflows such as:

- user administration
- role and permission administration
- plan administration
- plan feature administration
- company administration
- platform-level support and analytics

## Company Experience

Companies use NexoLocal as an operational workspace for:

- customers
- sales
- sales analytics
- inventory
- services
- suppliers
- finance
- finance containers
- recurring finance operations
- finance fund reset cycles
- cash operations
- main cash and subcash sessions
- active services
- business settings
- tax-related setup
- CSV exports for products and services
- CSV exports for customers
- PDF reports for sales, finance, and cash workflows
- segmented finance PDF reports with grouped subtotals
- consolidated cash reporting
- async pagination and filtering with partial HTML updates

## Public Documentation Scope

This document is intentionally limited to product-level capabilities. Internal routes, private callback flows, and implementation-specific operational details are not part of the public documentation set.
